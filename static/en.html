What is the Einbürgerungstest?

The German citizenship test (officially the "Leben in Deutschland" test) checks knowledge of the legal system, society, and the federal state you live in. It's a requirement for naturalization and is administered by the Federal Office for Migration and Refugees (BAMF).

In the real exam you get 33 questions: 30 general questions that apply nationwide, plus 3 questions specific to your federal state. You have 60 minutes and need at least 17 out of 33 correct answers to pass.

This app contains the complete official question pool — all 300 general questions plus the 10 state-specific questions for each of the 16 federal states.

Frequently asked questions

Is the Einbürgerungstest Trainer free?

Yes, it's completely free and open-source. No account, no ads, no tracking.

Are these the real exam questions?

Yes, all 460 official questions from the BAMF question pool are included — the 300 general questions plus the 10 questions for each of the 16 federal states.

How many questions do I need to answer correctly?

The real exam has 33 questions (30 general + 3 about your federal state). You pass with 17 or more correct answers, within 60 minutes.

Does the app work offline?

Yes. It can be installed as a Progressive Web App and then works without an internet connection.

Is the app available in German or Ukrainian too?

Yes, the app has a built-in language switcher for questions and explanations in German, English and Ukrainian. /en and /uk are just this landing page in those languages, not translated question sets.

Is this site official / affiliated with BAMF?

No. This is an independent, privately run practice project with no affiliation to the Federal Office for Migration and Refugees (BAMF) or any government agency.
